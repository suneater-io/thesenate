## Design Context

### Users

thesenate.io is primarily a personal expression, not an optimized conversion funnel. There is no target persona to design for. Visitors arrive through curiosity — a link, a referral, a search — and the site's job is to reward that curiosity, not to convert it into anything specific.

The desired outcome is not a form submission or a hire. It is a visitor who walks away wanting to know more. The range — Solution Architect, drummer, collector, Detroit, Counter-Strike — should land as genuinely surprising, not packaged. Nothing is pre-digested for the visitor's comfort.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **earned, heavy, precise.**

The brand belongs to Grant Hansen. It operates across two registers simultaneously — raw, dark, metal-adjacent; and systematic, architectural, competence-driven. These are not in conflict. The tension between them *is* the identity.

Voice: terse, declarative, real. Short sentences. No fluff. No exclamation marks. No "passionate about" language. Copy sounds like it came from somewhere — because it did.

Tagline: *Everything. At full volume.*

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual world**: Heavy music / metal aesthetics. The SENATE wordmark is a death metal illustration by SlayThyName — organic dripping letterforms, blade-like spikes, curved horns. The site lives in the same visual territory: industrial weight, darkness as a natural environment (not a style choice), typographic precision alongside raw graphic force.

**Theme**: Dark only. Near-black deep navy base (`#060810`), never pure black, never light. No light mode. No white backgrounds. No white cards.

**What it is not**:
- Not cyberpunk (no neon, no circuit-board motifs)
- Not gothic-romantic (no ornate flourishes)
- Not corporate dark mode (no smooth rounded-card design language)
- Not a standard portfolio (no clean, expected structure)
- Not performative darkness — the aesthetic reflects something real

**Color system**: Per-page accent held in `--blue` token. Each page has a distinct character color (steel blue, cold steel, amber copper, slate, burnt orange, slate blue, pale steel). All derived from cool-to-neutral ranges except Professional's amber. Never default Tailwind palette. Red is reserved for logo only.

**Typography**: Three fonts, fixed roles, no substitution.
- Barlow Condensed — display, headings, large callouts
- Chakra Petch — body, UI, card text
- Space Mono — labels, nav, mono data, eyebrow text

**Atmosphere**: Grain overlay on all pages (SVG fractalNoise, `opacity: 0.11`, fixed). Per-page ambient canvas effects (snow, rain, constellations, hex data stream, crane lights). Effects are environmental — they set room temperature, never compete with content.

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint over decoration.** The darkness is the environment, not the effect. Use space, weight, and type to create hierarchy. Atmospheric effects exist at low opacity and never obstruct. Add only what earns its place.

2. **Tension as a feature.** The site lives at the intersection of industrial metal aesthetics and architectural precision. Don't resolve the tension — use it. A section can feel like a blueprint *and* like a setlist.

3. **Reveal, don't explain.** Information is earned through exploration. Layer content behind interactions. The range of identities (architect, drummer, collector, Detroit) should create cognitive surprise, not be packaged into a tidy personal brand narrative.

4. **Every element has a job.** Nothing decorative that doesn't serve atmosphere or structure. No filler copy. No emoji. No iconography used as ornamentation. Phosphor icons only where they clarify function.

5. **Atmosphere is ambient, content is primary.** Canvas effects, grain, orbs, gradients — these set the mood. The moment any effect requires the user to notice it, it has failed. Reduce opacity until it disappears, then bring it back 30%.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Default Tailwind palette (indigo-500, blue-600, slate-300)
- `transition-all` — only animate `transform` and `opacity`
- Light surfaces or white backgrounds in any context
- Purple, green, or red hero tints (accent must match page `--blue`)
- Cards nested inside cards
- Generic card grid layouts (icon + heading + text, repeated identically)
- Gradient text or glassmorphism effects
- Hero metrics / big-number layouts as decoration
- Exclamation marks or buzzword copy
- System fonts, Inter, Roboto, Arial
