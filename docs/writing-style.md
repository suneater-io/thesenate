# Grant Hansen — Writing Style Guide

*Derived from raw Q&A answers, finalized creative copy across thesenate.io, and the grant-hansen.md document. This is observed style, not invented rules.*

---

## The Core

Terse. Declarative. Real. Short sentences that carry weight, not filler. The voice sounds like someone who thinks before he speaks, then says exactly what he means and stops.

Three words: **earned, heavy, precise.**

---

## Sentence Structure

### Short declaratives as anchors

Every section opens or closes with a sentence that could stand alone.

> "Music is not something I do. It's what I am."
> "I've played this game since I was 13. I haven't stopped."
> "The sell side funds the addiction."
> "That's where I belong."
> "Nothing has come close."

These aren't conclusions. They're statements of fact. No build-up, no hedging.

### Fragments used on purpose

When the subject is clear, drop it. This creates percussion — the same rhythm as the music.

> "Not the shooting. Everything else."
> "All personal."
> "Mentally and physically."
> "Been in rotation ever since."
> "Deep playoff runs. Conference final Game 7s. Chicago."

The fragment is not laziness. It's emphasis. Each word carries more when it stands alone.

### Rapid-fire sequences

When recounting action, drop subjects and conjunctions. Let the sequence move.

> "I played them One by Metallica. Explained exactly why I couldn't do it without the pedals. How they worked. Why I needed them. They bought them."

No "and then" or "so". Just event, event, event, result.

### Lists punctuated with periods, not commas

Comma lists are passive. Period lists hit harder.

> "eBay. Depop. Instagram. Local thrift."
> "Laimbeer. Isiah. Dumars."
> "Chaotic, loud, odd, different."

Use commas only when the items belong together. Use periods when each one deserves a moment.

---

## Voice

### Always first person

No passive voice. No "one might argue". Always "I".

> "I want the Pistons or the Wings to be the first."
> "I get right in the guts and experience it the way it should be experienced."

### Australian, not neutral

Preserve the natural phrasing. Never iron it out.

- "mum and dad" not "mom and dad"
- "I reckon" when genuinely approximate
- "a bit" as a natural qualifier
- "Hell yes" not "absolutely"

### Opinions stated plainly

No hedged opinions. If something is the best, say it's the best.

> "Still my number one. Nothing has come close."
> "The most violent, precise album thrash metal ever made."
> "The GIII Carl Banks Pistons 3x Champions jacket is probably my favorite piece."

"Probably" appears once in that last example — not from politeness, from genuine uncertainty. That's the only acceptable hedge. Use it when you actually aren't sure, not to soften a strong claim.

### Honest approximations

Don't overclaim precision. Don't underclaim either.

> "I would have been 9."
> "Five to ten times a year."
> "Around 2008."
> "12 or 13."

These feel more real than exact numbers because they're how he actually remembers things.

---

## Specificity

This is non-negotiable. Never generalize when a specific name or detail exists.

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "a Metallica album" | "Metallica Garage Inc on CD, a big wooden Metallica poster, and my own boombox" |
| "saw a big show" | "Cryptopsy in Melbourne. Flo Mournier" |
| "a vintage Pistons jacket" | "GIII Carl Banks Pistons 3x Champions jacket" |
| "a band I was in" | "Parkway Drive, I Killed the Prom Queen, The Red Shore" |

Proper nouns are not explained. If you know, you know. If you don't, Google it. Never add context that softens the specificity.

---

## Emotion

### Understate it

The emotion is in the detail, not the adjective.

> "Kids jumping from the stage into piles of people. I'll never forget it."

Not: "It was an incredible, unforgettable crowd experience."

The detail does the work. Let it.

### Never over-explain a feeling

> "I can't talk about it."
> "Hell yes."
> "It makes me proud every time."

Say how you feel. Stop. Don't explain why you feel that way unless the explanation adds something new.

### Complexity is honest, not a weakness

> "Absolutely heartbreaking. But that's the thing. Heartbreak that deep only happens when it means that much."

Mixed feelings are not indecision. State the contradiction directly. Don't resolve it into a tidy lesson.

---

## Paragraph Structure

No topic sentence, support, conclusion. Just momentum.

Paragraphs are 1 to 4 sentences. Each one earns its place. Nothing summarizes what came before.

The finisher is often the strongest line — but it's never a moral. It's a statement of fact or feeling.

> "I would rather go blind than go deaf."
> "Nothing has come close."
> "I still have it."
> "I'll never forget it."

---

## Tense

Past tense for narrative. Present tense for current state and things that are still true.

Mix them naturally within the same passage.

> "I put it on and nothing was ever the same. It was so heavy. Like nothing I'd heard before."  *(past narrative)*
> "Still my number one. Nothing has come close."  *(present truth)*

Don't stay in one tense when the other fits better. Trust the instinct.

---

## What Never Appears

These are hard rules. Not guidelines.

- **Em dashes** — never. Commas or a new sentence instead.
- **"Additionally", "Furthermore", "Moreover"** — no transitions that announce themselves
- **"Passionate about", "excited about", "committed to"** — promotional language, not his voice
- **Summary sentences** — never restate what was just said
- **Inspirational closers** — "and that's what makes it special", "the journey continues", "only time will tell"
- **Passive voice** — always name the subject
- **Rule of three in lists** — two is often enough; three can feel constructed
- **"Serves as", "stands as", "represents"** — always prefer "is"
- **Exclamation marks** — never in prose

## Examples of the Voice Working

**Anchoring a section:**
> "Music is not something I do. It's what I am."

**Building a narrative with fragments:**
> "A friend's birthday party. He had a kit, nobody there could play it. I basically spent the next few hours working it out."

**Specificity doing the emotional work:**
> "Just days after my 18th birthday I saw Cryptopsy in Melbourne. Flo Mournier was a huge influence to me at the time. Our band manager Storm basically crowd surfed to the front to make sure she caught his drum stick when he threw it. She brought it back to me. I still have it."

**Period lists for percussion:**
> "Howe. Yzerman. Federov. Delvecchio. Shanahan. Chelios. Probert. Konstantinov. Draper. Now Larkin."

**Understatement landing harder than drama:**
> "We parted ways the right way though. One final show. People moshing everywhere. Crowd surfers. Kids jumping from the stage into piles of people. I'll never forget it."

**The honest finish:**
> "Thirty years in, I explain music to people like medicine. Some people have something they need every day to keep them alive, keep them sane, in control, healthy. That's music for me. Without my music, there is no me. It connects to everything I do and am. Complex, fast, heavy. Sometimes it's sad. Sometimes angry. Sometimes it lifts you off the ground. But it's always something. I would rather go blind than go deaf."
