# Blog Article Ideas

Each post follows the existing pattern: one specific, real thing → something unexpected extracted from it.
Cross-domain transfers. No filler. Terse.

---

## Ready to Write

**The First Tattoo**
Meshuggah. Why that band, why that early, what it cost to commit to something that specific at that age. The difference between liking something and tattooing it on your body is the same as the difference between having an opinion and having a position.

**The 486**
Your parents' computer. The specific moment a machine became something you understood from the inside. Where the obsession actually started — not with AI, not with enterprise tech, with a beige box in a family home.

**What a Drummer Hears**
Everyone else in the room is listening to the song. The drummer is listening to the structure underneath it. Same concert, completely different experience. What that shift in perception is, and where else it shows up.

**100 Hats**
What you actually do when you collect something seriously — the hunt, the condition grading, the context research, the moment you decide a piece is worth keeping. It's not accumulation. It's curation with a specific thesis. What that thesis is.

**Meshuggah Is a Time Signature Argument**
Polyrhythm as a philosophy. Why Meshuggah hits different than everything else — not heavier, not faster, just operating on a different clock. What it means to function correctly while everything around you is in a different time.

**The Band Story**
"I'll tell that story another time." Tell it. A band that got somewhere. What you learned from being in a room with people who are all committed to the same difficult thing.

**What IGLs Actually Do**
The economy round covered resource management. This one covers the harder part — reading people in real-time under pressure and making calls they'll follow. Why some people get looked to in the room and others don't.

**Why I Still Watch the Lions**
The Detroit loyalty piece covered the unconditional logic. This one gets specific — a particular game, a particular loss, why you stayed in the seat. The micro version of the macro argument.

## Needs More Distance (Write Later)

**The First Time I Was Right About Something Nobody Believed**
A call you made — in tech, in CS, in a room at work — that got dismissed and turned out to be correct. What that experience actually teaches you versus what people say it teaches you.

**What Star Wars Lego Is Actually About**
Not nostalgia. The specific satisfaction of a constrained build system where the instructions are clear and the outcome is knowable. When everything else is ambiguous, why that matters.

**Detroit Wins**
The other side of the loyalty piece. The Wings dynasty. What it felt like when the team you chose unconditionally started winning championships. Whether that changes the relationship or just confirms it.
