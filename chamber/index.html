ABOUT

Most people manage chaos. They organize around it, wait for it to settle. Grant reads it.

The ability isn't technical. It's perceptual. A broken architecture, a losing round, a year that cost more than it gave back — the surface changes, the problem doesn't. Chaos has a shape. Systems don't fail randomly; they fail along the same seams, every time. Pull back far enough and the line through becomes obvious. The work is learning to do that before the noise convinces you there isn't one.

Music came first — not the kind that's easy to like, but some of the most complex and technical music out there. The kind most people never get past the surface of. When you spend years tracking everything happening inside music that demands that level of attention, you stop hearing things one layer at a time. Counter‑Strike after — thirteen rounds of incomplete information at high stakes. Norse mythology, Detroit, vintage — different worlds, same thing: stay in it when it's costing you, see what everyone else has given up on, build what actually ships.

Everything here was built alone. The record, the systems, the disciplines. Not as a condition. As a choice.

DISCIPLINES / 06
01
Music

Drums before everything else. Hold the framework while everything around you moves. The right record doesn't get found by browsing — you find it by showing up every time.

02
Detroit

Wings. Lions. Tigers. Pistons. Losing seasons as much as winning ones. What you stay loyal to when it costs you something is the only loyalty that counts.

03
Norse

Odin traded an eye for wisdom. Hung from Yggdrasil nine days to earn knowledge. The Eddas aren't religion — they're a manual for what things cost. Ragnarök is coming. The gods know it and fight anyway.

04
Counter‑Strike

Sixteen rounds, five players, incomplete information. A 1v3 defuse with one bullet left — panic is the only thing that finishes you before the enemy does. Staying locked when the model says you're losing migrates off-screen.

05
Vintage

A 1994 Wings Starter jacket is a primary source document. Vintage is archaeology in fabric form — what you carry tells a story biography can't. Each piece earns its place or it doesn't.

06
Architecture

Every other discipline feeds this one. Architecture is where all of it becomes a blueprint — and then a result. The one that ships.
