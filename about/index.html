About

Grant Hansen
Grant
Hansen

I'm a Solution Architect. I live for my kids. I bleed Detroit. That's the short version.

The longer version starts with a 486 in my parents' house. It runs through a band at 15 playing stages with Parkway Drive and I Killed the Prom Queen, a label deal signed before the album that never came, drums I never put down, and a career I built from scratch. Everything was earned. None of it was given.

The same person in the middle of a pit on a Wednesday night is the same person in the room with the CEO. I don't change who I am. I just know how to find the human part of anyone.

Meshuggah at 14 was my Matrix moment. The music hit me but the philosophy hit harder. Something could exist that broke every rule and be better for it. Rules are starting points. You learn them, then you cross the line. But only when crossing it gets you somewhere that couldn't exist before. That's how I think about everything.

I love being pushed out of my comfort zone. Not as a discipline. As a feeling. The most extreme music, the most uncomfortable films, the hardest decisions, the highest pressure moments. Most people won't go there. That's exactly why it's worth going.

I have over 50 hours in the tattoo chair. Both arm sleeves, left leg, stomach, chest. The Meshuggah logo at 18 was first. Then I waited until 30. Told myself full sleeves were a commitment I needed to be sure about. When I was 30 I knew. Norse mythology, music, Star Wars, horror. The left arm is Ragnarök in Norse style illustration, written in runes, with Jörmungandr wrapping the whole arm.

Ragnarök is the end of everything. The gods know it's coming. They fight anyway. For me that's not a story about loss. It's a story about what gets born from it. I've faced fights I thought I might lose. Some I did. But something new always came from the other side. The important part was never the outcome. It was taking the fight and not fearing it.

At work I collaborate, plan, strategise and design. I also bring smiles and laughs. Both matter equally. Hard problems in AI, enterprise technology, integrated health. But what people come to me for is direction, inspiration and knowing where to go next. What I want people to say when I'm not in the room: he was fun to work with and we always did great work. That's it.

My great-grandparents came from Norway. That's where the thread started. I dug into Norse mythology through that connection and never stopped. I don't believe in religion or gods. But I believe in nature, in treating the world with respect, in kindness to the things around you. That's what Norse paganism was founded on. It's where the peace comes from.

My kids ground me. My personality runs wild and they tame it. They've taught me more about myself than I've ever taught them. They're my best friends. They just don't know it yet.

WHAT I BELIEVE
01
Earned, not given

I've been in dark places. I've made mistakes that nearly cost me everything. The fact that I'm still here, still building, is the point. You can't feel the weight of success without knowing what failure actually costs. Not as a concept. As something with a real cost attached to it.

02
Rules exist to be broken

Not for rebellion. For evolution. Meshuggah broke every rule in music and arrived somewhere that couldn't have existed within them. Learn the rules well enough to know which ones to cross, then cross them on purpose. In technology, in strategy, in every room you walk into.

03
Find what's underneath

Computers aren't click-and-open things. Problems aren't what they look like on the surface. People aren't their first impression. I've always pulled at things to find the layer nobody else bothered looking for. That instinct, how does this actually work, runs through everything I've done.
